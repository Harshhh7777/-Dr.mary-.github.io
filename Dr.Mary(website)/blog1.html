Eminent Doctors and Practitioners of Natural Health Science

Many doctors and practitioners of medicine from different parts of the world have come out independently to declare that taking medicines is not improving our health, but it is actually ruining our health. They declared a war against medicine and advocated Natural Health Science as the only way to solve all our health problems.

Some famous natural health science practitioners include Dr. Tilden, Dr. Isaac Jennings, Dr. Henry Linder, Sylvester Graham, Arnold Russell, Louis Kunhe, Dr. T.C. Fry, Herbert Shelton, and many others. Each of these practitioners had unique contributions to Natural Health Science, such as:

Dr. Tilden: A visionary ahead of his time, Dr. Tilden boldly questioned the prevailing medical norms and championed the use of natural remedies and healthy lifestyle choices. He recognized the body's inherent capacity to heal itself when provided with the right tools and advocated for a holistic understanding of wellness.

Dr. Isaac Jennings: An influential figure in Natural Health Science, Dr. Jennings introduced the groundbreaking concept of the no-breakfast plan. By encouraging individuals to allow their bodies to rest and rejuvenate before consuming food in the morning, he emphasized the importance of aligning our habits with the body's natural rhythms.

Dr. Henry Linder: Dr. Linder's contributions to natural health were centered around the powerful practice of fasting. He believed that fasting provided the body with an opportunity to cleanse and regenerate, leading to improved overall health and vitality.

Sylvester Graham: A visionary of his time, Graham highlighted the significance of a whole foods, plant-based diet for optimal health. He promoted the consumption of natural, unprocessed foods, and is best known for creating the wholesome Graham cracker as a nutritious alternative to the unhealthy snacks of his era.

Arnold Russell: Russell's advocacy for natural health emphasized the importance of a balanced lifestyle encompassing regular physical exercise, exposure to fresh air, and proper nutrition. He encouraged individuals to take responsibility for their own well-being and embrace the power of self-healing.

Louis Kuhne: Kuhne's groundbreaking work, "The New Science of Healing," challenged conventional medical practices and shed light on the body's natural healing abilities. His emphasis on hydrotherapy as a therapeutic treatment and the importance of maintaining a clean internal environment resonated deeply with those seeking natural solutions.

Dr. T.C. Fry: Dr. Fry played a pivotal role in promoting the benefits of a raw food diet. His teachings focused on the detrimental effects of cooking on the nutritional content of food and emphasized the consumption of fresh fruits, vegetables, and raw plant-based foods for optimal health.

Herbert Shelton: Shelton's significant contributions centered around natural hygiene and fasting. He advocated for a clean and healthy lifestyle, emphasizing the body's inherent ability to heal itself when provided with the right conditions.

These doctors and practitioners, along with many others, have made significant contributions to Natural Health Science. Their work has provided valuable insights into the holistic approach to health and well-being, inspiring countless individuals to embrace natural remedies and healthy living habits.
